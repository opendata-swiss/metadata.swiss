## Publish data[](https://handbook.opendata.swiss/en/content/publizieren/publizieren.html#daten-publizieren)

In this chapter, we explain how to publish Open Government Data. Please note the following points:

* opendata.swiss is a metadata catalog. It refers to published Open Government Data in a standardized, easy-to-understand, and reusable form, thereby increasing its visibility.
* The [OGD office](https://handbook.opendata.swiss/en/content/glossar/begriffe.html#term-Geschaftsstelle-Open-Government-Data-OGD) is responsible for controlling the entire catalog. However, it is the responsibility of the publishing organizations or individuals to ensure that legal, technical, and organizational requirements are met, see [«OGD Guidelines»](https://handbook.opendata.swiss/en/content/glossar/bibliothek/ogd-richtlinien.html).
* For the first publication, a test run is carried out in the [«acceptance environment»](https://handbook.opendata.swiss/en/content/glossar/begriffe.html#term-Abnahmeumgebung) of opendata.swiss for all publication variants. This gives data-publishing organizations or individuals the opportunity to extensively test their datasets and all further information and make any necessary changes. From the second publication onwards, data publishers can independently publish new data. The acceptance environment is available at any time for test publications.
* User management is handled centrally by the [OGD office](https://handbook.opendata.swiss/en/content/glossar/begriffe.html#term-Geschaftsstelle-Open-Government-Data-OGD). New users or adjustments to existing permissions must therefore always be reported to us.

We guide first-time publishers step-by-step through the process. In the first step, the [OGD office](https://handbook.opendata.swiss/en/content/glossar/begriffe.html#term-Geschaftsstelle-Open-Government-Data-OGD) must register the new organization and users on opendata.swiss. Only then can the publication process start.

If you are already registered as a user on opendata.swiss, you can skip directly to the content for registered users. There you can directly select the appropriate publication variant for your data.
